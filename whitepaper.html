Executive Summary

NaloDAO is a decentralized autonomous organization (DAO) built to fund, verify, and reward regenerative action on Earth.

We exist at the intersection of blockchain technology and ecological restoration—using transparent, community-governed systems to direct capital toward projects that heal land, strengthen communities, and restore living systems.

Unlike traditional organizations, NaloDAO operates without centralized control. Decisions are made collectively by contributors through on-chain voting. Impact is verified transparently through real-world evidence. Value flows to those doing the work—not extracted by intermediaries.

Our Mission: To create a regenerative economy where caring for Earth is not just valued—it's rewarded.
Our Vision: A global network of bioregional communities coordinating resources, sharing knowledge, and governing commons-based systems that prioritize life over profit.

The Problem: Broken Systems

The Extractive Economy

Our current economic system is designed for extraction, not regeneration:

  • Soil is disappearing faster than it can regenerate
  • Billions of dollars flow to climate conferences with minimal ground-level impact
  • Regenerative leaders struggle to access even microgrants
  • Ecosystems are sacrificed for quarterly profits
  • Communities with deep ecological knowledge remain underfunded and underrepresented

Misaligned Incentives

The problem isn't lack of solutions. We know how to:

  • Reforest degraded land
  • Restore coral reefs
  • Build regenerative food systems
  • Decentralize governance

The issue is that incentives are misaligned:

  • Funding is top-down and bureaucratic
  • Verification is gatekept by institutions
  • Impact is obscured by paperwork and PR
  • Capital pools at the top instead of circulating through communities

The Need for New Infrastructure

Traditional systems cannot solve problems they were designed to create. We need:

  • Transparent funding that reaches grassroots efforts directly
  • Verifiable impact tracked on-chain, not in closed reports
  • Community governance where stakeholders have real voice
  • Circular economies where value flows back to regenerators

The Solution: Regenerative Coordination

What is Regeneration?

Regeneration goes beyond sustainability. Where sustainability asks "how do we do less harm?", regeneration asks "how can we restore, renew, and enliven?"

Regeneration is:

  • Cyclic, not linear — Everything returns to the source
  • Resilient, not rigid — Feedback loops adapt and evolve
  • Diverse and decentralized — Monocultures collapse; networks thrive
  • Collaborative, not competitive — Mutual aid, symbiosis, cooperation
"These principles apply to gardens and governance alike. A forest doesn't have a CEO. It has emergent order. So can our economies."

The Role of Technology

Blockchain, when used with intention, offers:

  • Transparency — Where funds go, how votes are cast, what outcomes are achieved
  • Incentivization — Tokens that reward real-world impact
  • Coordination — Decentralized governance where every stakeholder has voice
  • Verification — On-chain proof of regenerative action

But technology alone is not enough. It must be rooted in place, guided by wisdom, and wielded with care.

What is NaloDAO?

Origin Story

NaloDAO was born from the soil—in compost piles on small islands, in conversations under mango trees, and in the quiet knowing that our future depends on communities reclaiming the power to regenerate.

The name "Nalo" means "wave" or "to float" in several languages—a fitting metaphor for a project born at the edge of land and sea, designed to flow resources where they're needed most.

Definition

NaloDAO is a regenerative coordination system that:

  1. Governs Funds — DAO members vote on where funding goes
  2. Issues Tokens — Creates NALO tokens for governance and rewards
  3. Verifies Impact — Uses on-ground evidence to confirm real outcomes
  4. Rewards Regeneration — Pays contributors for ecological and social work
  5. Connects Communities — Hosts platforms and physical hubs for collaboration

Core Philosophy: Three Ethics

NaloDAO is guided by three ethical pillars rooted in permaculture:

1. 🌍 Earth Care

Regenerating the Source

Earth is not a backdrop to the economy—it is the foundation of all wealth.

Earth Care means:

  • Protecting and restoring ecosystems
  • Designing with nature, not against it
  • Honoring land as a living being

In NaloDAO's economy, Earth is an active stakeholder. Projects that heal land, water, and air are prioritized. Impact is measured in trees planted, topsoil built, and biodiversity restored—not profit margins.

2. 🤝 People Care

Empowering the Stewards

Healthy systems depend on healthy people.

People Care means:

  • Supporting local leadership and cultural wisdom
  • Prioritizing equity and access
  • Ensuring all participants feel seen, heard, and valued

NaloDAO is designed for inclusivity: mobile-first tools, language accessibility, onboarding for non-tech users, and funding models that compensate not just coders, but cooks, caregivers, and composters.

3. ⚖️ Fair Share

Rebalancing the Flow

Fair Share is about equity, not charity.

Fair Share asks:

  • Who holds power?
  • Who receives value?
  • How do we design for circulation instead of concentration?

NaloDAO's economic model includes:

  • Transparent token distribution
  • Regenerative reinvestment mechanisms
  • Cooperative ownership structures
  • Capped gains to prevent wealth concentration

Fair Share doesn't mean everyone gets the same—it means everyone gets enough to thrive, contribute, and grow.

The NALO Token

What is NALO?

NALO is not just currency—it's a tool for coordination, reward, and regeneration.

The NALO token (represented as $NALO in crypto convention) serves three primary functions:

  1. 🗳️ Governance — Vote on proposals, funding decisions, and protocol changes
  2. 💰 Rewards — Earn tokens for verified regenerative contributions
  3. 🔑 Access — Unlock resources, tools, and community benefits

Token Design Principles

NALO is designed with regenerative economics in mind:

  • Utility-first — Value tied to real-world outcomes, not speculation
  • Slow growth — Designed for stability and long-term value
  • Circular flow — Tokens circulate through the ecosystem, not extracted
  • Earned value — Distributed through contribution, not just capital

How NALO Flows: The Regenerative Cycle

The circular economy in action:

  1. FUNDING → Donors/Funders → Treasury
  2. DISTRIBUTION → Treasury → Approved Projects
  3. EXECUTION → Projects → On-Ground Work
  4. VERIFICATION → Evidence → Validation System
  5. REWARDS → Verified Impact → Contributors
  6. REINVESTMENT → Portion → Commons Fund
  7. CIRCULATION → Back to Step 1 (Continuous Loop)

This keeps value flowing through the ecosystem rather than concentrating at the top.

How NaloDAO Works

The Regenerative Loop

Step-by-step process:

  1. PROPOSE → Community member submits regenerative project
  2. VOTE → DAO members review and vote on proposal
  3. FUND → Approved projects receive NALO allocation
  4. ACT → Project team executes on-ground work
  5. VERIFY → Evidence submitted (photos, GPS, testimonials)
  6. REWARD → Verified impact triggers token distribution
  7. REINVEST → Portion flows back to commons fund

Verification System: Proof of Regeneration (PoR)

NaloDAO uses a multi-layered verification system:

Level 1: Self-Reporting

  • Photos and videos
  • GPS coordinates
  • Written documentation
  • Community testimonials

Level 2: Community Validation

  • Local node verification
  • Peer review process
  • Living Library confirmation

Level 3: Technical Verification

  • Satellite imagery analysis
  • Sensor data (soil, water, air)
  • Blockchain oracles
  • Third-party audits (for large projects)

Living Libraries: Grounding Digital in Physical

What is a Living Library?

A Living Library is a regenerative hub — a physical space where digital coordination meets on-ground action.

Think of it as:

  • A community garden + makerspace + learning center
  • A node in the global NaloDAO network
  • A place where regeneration is practiced, not just discussed

Functions of Living Libraries

Education

  • Workshops on permaculture, composting, water harvesting
  • Blockchain and DAO literacy training
  • Skill-sharing and mentorship programs

Production

  • Food forests and gardens
  • Compost systems
  • Seed libraries
  • Tool sharing

Coordination

  • Local DAO governance meetings
  • Project planning and collaboration
  • Community events and ceremonies

Verification

  • On-ground validation of local projects
  • Documentation and storytelling
  • Impact measurement

How to Start a Living Library

  1. Gather Community — Find 3-5 committed stewards
  2. Secure Land — Access to space (owned, leased, or commons)
  3. Submit Proposal — Apply for NaloDAO funding
  4. Build Infrastructure — Start with basics (compost, garden beds, meeting space)
  5. Connect Digitally — Set up wallets, join DAO platforms
  6. Document & Share — Submit proof of regeneration, earn rewards
  7. Grow Network — Connect with other Living Libraries globally

Technology Stack

Blockchain: Stellar

Why Stellar?

  • ⚡ Fast — 3-5 second transactions
  • 💰 Low cost — Fractions of a cent per transaction
  • 🌿 Energy efficient — Minimal environmental impact
  • 🌍 Built for financial inclusion — Designed for global access
  • 🔒 Secure and battle-tested — Proven technology

Smart Contracts

Automated agreements that execute when conditions are met:

  • Release funds when milestones verified
  • Distribute rewards based on contribution
  • Enforce governance decisions
  • Manage token economics

Oracles: Bridging Physical & Digital

Oracles bring real-world data on-chain:

  • GPS coordinates from field work
  • Photos and videos of projects
  • Sensor data (soil health, water quality)
  • Community validation signatures

Decentralized Identity (DID)

  • Verify participants without invading privacy
  • Build reputation over time
  • Enable trust without centralized databases

Governance Model

Decision-Making Process

1. Proposal Submission

  • Any member can submit a proposal
  • Requires minimum NALO stake
  • Must include clear objectives and budget

2. Discussion Period

  • Community reviews and provides feedback
  • Proposer can revise based on input
  • Typically 7-14 days

3. Voting Period

  • Token-weighted voting
  • Quorum requirements
  • Typically 3-7 days

4. Execution

  • Approved proposals automatically funded
  • Smart contracts release resources
  • Progress tracked transparently

Governance Circles

Core Circles:

  • 🌍 Land & Ecology — Project evaluation, verification standards
  • 💻 Technology — Platform development, security, infrastructure
  • 📚 Education — Curriculum, workshops, onboarding
  • 💰 Treasury — Financial management, token economics
  • 🤝 Community — Culture, conflict resolution, inclusion

Voting Power

Token-weighted with safeguards:

  • 1 NALO = 1 vote (base)
  • Contribution multipliers (verified impact increases weight)
  • Quadratic voting for key decisions (prevents plutocracy)
  • Delegation options (lend voting power to trusted members)

Checks & Balances

  • Veto power for critical decisions (requires supermajority)
  • Time locks on major changes (cooling-off periods)
  • Multi-sig treasury (requires multiple signatures for large withdrawals)
  • Emergency pause (community can halt operations if needed)
